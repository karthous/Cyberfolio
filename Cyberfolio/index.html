Part B

In part B, I finished the maps on our reserves page and campsites page individually with the government's API, leaflet.js, and terraformer.js. Leaflet is used to show the geojson data on the map. As some of the data from the government's datasets are in format of well-know text instead of geojson, Terraformer is used to convert well-known text data to genjson data. With the map, we can obtain the information about the name, location, and website of a destination if the information is provided by the government's dataset. If the information is not provided, it will show that the information is unknown. Besides, I did the presentation with one of my teammates.

Screenshot of a map on our pages

Portfolio

In this stage, I created this page with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It was named as Cyberfolio, because this page applied the cyberpunk style and it was used for my portfolio. To style this page, I researched the knowledge about cyberpunk and carefully selected the colors and fonts.
